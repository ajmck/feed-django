AskOtago

If you're looking for information about the University of Otago, visit ask.otago.ac.nz.

Critic was fortunate enough to purchase the domain names for AskOtago.com and AskOtago.co.nz before the University of Otago remembered to. So, we chucked together a few questions and a quick Photoshop jobbie, and ended up with this.

Not wanting to half-ass it, AskOtago.com has been upgraded, and now anyone can Ask Otago anything*. Post, comment, and vote to your hearts content.

{% comment %}

AskOtago is also a research project for SURV319 Geospatial Analysis and Programming, undertaken by Alex McKirdy. When you post or comment, you have the option of assigning a location nearby. What can we gather from this? Things like which street has better banter - Castle or Hyde? Does everyone use AskOtago at the end of their lectures? Is everything on this website fabricated in the Critic office?

Guidelines

You might have noticed the asterisk in the above paragraph. AskOtago is supposed to be some lighthearted fun (and subterfuge against the university). However, to stop it from being complete carnage, there are some ground rules.

  • Don't be a dick.
  • No personal information (names or contact details).
  • No racism, sexism, *phobias, (see rule one)

Note that content is filtered by Azure Content Moderator for the above reasons - flagged content will be manually reviewed by moderators.

We reserve the right to collect data for the purpose of analytics, logging, and the general functionality of the website.

This site relies on third party services and providers - namely Microsoft Azure, Heroku, Google Analytics.

Data Collection

Cookies: To ensure AskOtago functions correctly, cookies must be enabled and stored on your device.

Third Party Providers: This website relies on Google Analytics which may gather information about your usage of the site. Text data is submitted to Microsoft Azure Content Moderator once it reaches our server, in order to provide spam and explicit content filtering. Data is stored on our hosting provider (currently Heroku). External libraries such as jQuery, Bootstrap, and OpenStreetMap may also be loaded.

Data we collect:

  • IP Address
  • Device information
  • Browser information
  • Reported location (returned by your browser)
  • Entered location (if picking a custom location)

Privacy Policy: I mean, it's probably not compliant with EU regulations. End users may only access posts, comments, and vote summaries, and locations are only displayed in aggregate form.
